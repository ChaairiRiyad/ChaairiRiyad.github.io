Materials and methods used for the project

For this project, we relied on R Studio and Python to simplify the complex world of air pollution. Our first task was 'data cleaning'—a process where we removed errors and unnecessary details from our files so the computer could read the dates and times accurately. We also simplified the technical sensor data, turning confusing codes into clear information like wind speed and gas concentrations.

A key part of our work was putting two different datasets together: one for weather and another for pollution. This allowed us to see the full picture of how the environment affects the air we breathe. To finish, we used the openair library to create the visual charts on this site. Instead of just showing lists of numbers, we created heatmaps and graphs that make it easy to spot exactly when and why pollution levels change.

These steps set up the RStudio environment with the essential libraries for analyzing air quality data.

  1. install.packages(c("openair","tidyverse","lares"))
  2. library(tidyverse)
  3. library(dplyr)
  4. library(lares)
  5. library(openair)
