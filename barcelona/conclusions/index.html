Conclusions

Upon completion of this project, it is evident that even a vast and complex dataset can provide profound insights into Barcelona’s air quality. By systematically cleaning and structuring data from Meteocat and the XPVCA network(utilizing SIVIC protocols and government APIs)we successfully distilled raw data into identifiable pollution trends.

One interesting result is how daily human activity affects air pollution. The graphs of NO₂ show that pollution increases during the hours when people travel to work or school. This means that pollution peaks are closely related to traffic and daily routines in the city.

The results also show that although pollution levels change over the years, high values during rush hour are still very common. This is especially visible in dense areas such as the Sants district. Visualization tools like the openair library helped transform complex data tables into graphs that are easier to understand.

In conclusion, this project shows that programming and data analysis are very useful for studying environmental problems. Air quality in the city is strongly influenced by people's daily habits and mobility patterns.
